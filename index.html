    How age is counted

    An exact age is made up of three parts: full years, the extra months since the last birthday, and the leftover days since the last monthly anniversary. This calculator works out each part directly from the calendar, so it stays accurate even across leap years and months of different lengths.

    Knowing a precise age is useful for school enrollment cut-offs, eligibility checks, legal age requirements, or simply working out how many days old someone is for a milestone celebration.

    Why unit conversion matters

    Most countries use the metric system, but plenty of everyday situations still mix in imperial units — recipes in cups, road signs in miles, or body weight in pounds. Converting between them by hand is easy to get wrong, since the multipliers aren't round numbers (a mile is 1.609 kilometers, not a clean ratio).

    This converter covers the four unit families people look up most often: length, weight, volume and temperature, with results updating instantly as you type.

    Where random numbers come in handy

    A random number generator picks values with no predictable pattern, which makes it useful for raffle and giveaway draws, choosing who goes first in a game, picking a sample for quality checks, or generating test data for a spreadsheet or app.

    Turning on "no repeated numbers" treats the draw like pulling names from a hat — once a number is picked, it's removed from the pool so it can't come up again.

    Body Mass Index
    —

    Categories shown follow the standard WHO reference ranges: under 18.5 underweight, 18.5–24.9 typical, 25–29.9 above typical, 30+ well above typical. For personal health guidance, talk with a doctor.

    What BMI does and doesn't tell you

    Body Mass Index divides weight by height squared to give a quick, population-level screening number. It's widely used because it's simple to calculate, but it doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or body composition — so an athlete and a sedentary person of the same BMI can have very different health profiles.

    Use this calculator for a general reference point, not a diagnosis. A doctor or dietitian can give guidance that actually fits your situation.
